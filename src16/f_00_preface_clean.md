Assalamu alaikum. I almost didn't write this page. Every version I tried sounded like a foreword, and this book doesn't need a foreword. So I'll keep it short, and I mean that.

The chapters don't have to be read in order. Each one stands on its own, so if a title calls to you, start there. If you're not sure, start from the beginning; they build on each other. When a number appears next to a fact or figure in the text, the source is in the references at the back. Arabic terms are defined in the online glossary, also at the back.

This book will ask something of you. Not agreement, not certainty. Just enough honesty to read carefully, and enough patience to let something land before you argue with it. Some of what's here might disturb something you've been comfortable with. Some of it might answer a question you've been carrying for years without ever knowing how to ask it. A heart isn't helped only by being soothed. Sometimes it's helped by finally being told the truth. That's all this is trying to be.

If you get to the last page, you won't know everything about Islam. Nobody could promise you that. But you'll know where to stand: before Allah, with His guidance in front of you, His mercy still open to you, and your meeting with Him still ahead of you. After that, the question isn't whether there's a path. The question is whether you'll walk it.
